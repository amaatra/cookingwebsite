Cutting Vegetables

In this tutorial, you will learn about two ways to cut vegetables - julienning and mincing. With these skills in your arsenal, you will be able to cook almost anything!

Julienning: Julienning is when you cut a vegetable into thin, vertical strips, like a matchstick or a toothpick. To julienne a vegetable, you should first peel it if it has any skin, and cut it into smaller pieces, about 6cm/2in. Cut these pieces in half so they lay flat against the cutting board, then cut it vertically into thin, matchstick-shaped pieces.

Mincing: Mincing means to cut something into very small pieces. To mince a vegetable, first cut it in half and remove any ends with leaves or stems. Then, cut the vegetable into thin vertical slices, about 1cm/0.5in wide. After this, line up all the slices and cut the vegetable perpendicularly, to form small cubes. Finally, gather up the small pieces with either the knife or your hands. Place one hand on the handle of the knife and the other hand on the dull end, and rock the knife back and forth over the pile until you have many small pieces.
